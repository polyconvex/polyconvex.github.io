Type: Blue supergiant star

Location: Constellation Orion

Temperature: Around 11,000 to 12,000 Kelvin

Description: DAG is one of the brightest stars in the night sky and the brightest star in the constellation Orion.

Its blue-white color is a result of its high surface temperature, following Wien's displacement law, which states that the peak wavelength of a black body radiation curve is inversely proportional to its temperature.

Hotter stars emit more light at shorter wavelengths, which corresponds to blue and white light.
